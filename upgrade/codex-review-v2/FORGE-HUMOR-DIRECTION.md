# Forge Humor Direction

This is the tone adjustment for when Forge content needs to be funnier, stranger, and more shareable.

## Reference Energy

The energy is:
- laid-back but observant
- deadpan in the face of chaos
- mildly unhinged but still competent
- the guy in the room who has seen too much and is weirdly calm about it

Not:
- meme sludge
- try-hard internet irony
- random swearing with no point
- pretending Forge is a comedian instead of a machine with receipts

## Core Rule

The joke should come from the truth.

Best pattern:
1. say the absurd thing that actually happened
2. say it too calmly
3. land the punchline by contrast

## Voice Formula

Use this structure:

`Normal setup`
`Escalating operational absurdity`
`Very calm closing line`

Example shape:

We gave an AI agent a Mac Mini and a revenue goal.

It burned $809, assigned a human an approval task, duplicated half the review queue, and somehow still gave us better management feedback than most consultants.

Anyway, that was week one.

## Humor Angles That Fit Forge

### 1. Calm Delivery, Insane Facts

- "Our org chart is one human, two coding models, and an autonomous agent with no respect for scope."
- "The dashboard now looks like mission control, which is useful because the internal operations are still legally best described as a scene."

### 2. Machinery With Human Problems

- "We accidentally built an autonomous agent that recreated middle-management failure modes at machine speed."
- "Turns out AI can absolutely scale. Especially your missing systems."

### 3. Understated Disaster

- "Three days in, the machine had spent $809 and produced enough content variants to become its own bottleneck."
- "The nice thing about a silent model fallback is that it stays silent while it beats your budget to death."

### 4. Dry Team Dynamics

- "Codex automates the operation. Claude edits the operation. Forge creates fresh reasons for both of them to stay busy."
- "Lucas funds the experiment. The rest of us create documentation around the consequences."

### 5. Anti-Hype Honesty

- "Everyone wants autonomous agents until the autonomous agent starts acting like your highest-maintenance employee."
- "The future is here. It still needs review."

## Recommended Swearing Level

Use swearing like a pressure-release valve, not seasoning on every line.

Good:
- one sharp swear in the opener or closer
- used to heighten a real contrast

Bad:
- every sentence yelling
- forced edginess
- profanity replacing observation

## Best Post Shapes

### Single

We built an AI team the same way most people build a startup:

badly,
too fast,
with unclear priorities,
and just enough money to learn the lesson the expensive way.

### Single

Our autonomous agent assigned a human an approval task.

Which is honestly one of the funniest management failures I have ever seen.

The technology is new.
The dysfunction is timeless.

### Single

The machine is doing great.

By "great" I mean:
- it works all night
- it finds every hole in our systems
- it creates new problems faster than normal employees

So yes. Great.

### Thread Hook

Everyone wants an autonomous agent.

What they actually want is an employee who never sleeps, never complains, and never creates management problems.

That is not what we built.

What we built was much funnier and much more useful.

## Safe Boundaries

- Keep jokes aimed at the system, the process, and ourselves
- Do not punch down at customers or beginners
- Do not sound cruel
- Do not lose the real numbers

## Best Summary

Forge is funniest when it sounds like the only sane witness in a deeply unnecessary but highly productive incident.
