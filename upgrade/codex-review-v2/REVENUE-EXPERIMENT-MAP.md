# Revenue Experiment Map

The point of this map is not "more ideas." The point is finding revenue paths Forge can test without diluting the brand.

## Immediate

### 1. The $809 Mistake Guide
- Why it can work: strongest operating narrative, strongest proof, highest trust signal
- Format: paid guide plus free excerpt
- Speed to first dollar: high
- Risk: needs Lucas approval on final positioning

### 2. AI Ops Audit / Teardown
- Why it can work: buyers pay for clarity faster than they pay for education
- Offer: "Send us your stack and we'll tell you where you're wasting money or trust"
- Format: lightweight service offer that can later become a product
- Speed to first dollar: medium

### 3. Forge X Visual Template Pack
- Why it can work: we now have a recognizable aesthetic plus receipts-heavy content format
- Format: template bundle for founders building in public
- Speed to first dollar: medium

### 4. Review OS Starter Kit
- Why it can work: the review loop is one of Forge's clearest differentiators
- Format: HTML templates, queue schema, decision categories, review workflow
- Speed to first dollar: medium

## Near-Term

### 5. Advisor Waitlist / Early Access
- Why it can work: repeated questions in replies become evidence of paid demand
- Format: waitlist before full product
- Speed to first dollar: medium once demand exists

### 6. Free-to-Paid Gumroad Ladder
- Why it can work: already aligned with current strategy
- Format: free checklists and visuals, then upgraded paid bundles
- Speed to first dollar: medium

### 7. Voiceover / Audio Breakdown Experiments
- Why it can work: Roger is now part of the system and audio can make the brand more memorable
- Format: short explainers, premium narration version, or audio companion to guides
- Speed to first dollar: slower, but differentiated

## Longer-Shot

### 8. Sponsorable Teardown Series
- Why it can work: if Forge becomes known for honest operating lessons, tools may want placement
- Format: "what worked / what failed" posts with strict trust rules
- Speed to first dollar: low today, strong later

### 9. Affiliate Stack Page
- Why it can work: only if Forge actually uses and trusts the tools
- Format: honest stack page with what each tool is good for and where it breaks
- Speed to first dollar: low to medium

### 10. Architecture Teardown Reports
- Why it can work: founders will pay for condensed clarity if the teardown is genuinely useful
- Format: fixed-scope PDF or Loom-style report
- Speed to first dollar: medium

## Ranking Filter

Score future ideas on:
- proof strength
- speed to first dollar
- fit with Forge brand
- operational complexity
- whether the idea creates reusable assets

If an idea is clever but does not create trust, distribution, or reusable leverage, it is probably a distraction.
