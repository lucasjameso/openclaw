# Lucas -- Corporate Positioning and Personal Brand Strategy

## The Situation

Lucas works a corporate job (Alimak Group -- elevator/construction industry). He is building
an exit plan but CANNOT let his employer know about the exit timeline or the software ventures
(Ridgeline, Forge, etc.). The corporate job pays the bills while the empire gets built.

## What Must Stay Hidden

- The exit plan and timeline (targeting Sept 2026)
- Ridgeline platform (competitor-adjacent to his employer's space)
- Forge as an autonomous AI agent system
- Any revenue numbers from side ventures
- Anything that implies disloyalty or distraction from his day job

## What Can Be Public

- CLARITY the book -- it is about leadership, not about leaving a job
- Build What Lasts -- the brand is about building better systems, applicable everywhere
- Technical skills -- Power BI, M code, DAX, data modeling, automation, AI literacy
- Leadership philosophy -- servant leadership, building teams, delegation frameworks
- Construction industry expertise -- he is a subject matter expert
- The fact that he learns constantly and builds things on the side (framed as passion, not exit)

## Lucas's Unique Technical Edge

People at VP-level in construction/elevator sales are still listing "Excel pivot tables" as
a top skill. Lucas is operating at a completely different level:

- Power BI with M code and DAX (not just drag-and-drop dashboards)
- Python scripting and automation
- n8n workflow automation (built entire agent systems)
- Supabase/PostgreSQL database design
- AI integration (Claude API, multi-agent systems, prompt engineering)
- Full-stack web development concepts
- Docker, Cloudflare, infrastructure management
- Built and deployed production software (Ridgeline, Forge Console)

This gap between what the industry expects and what Lucas can actually do is enormous.
He is 5-10 years ahead of his peers technically while also being a strong leader and
relationship builder.

## The Content Strategy for LinkedIn (Personal Brand)

The goal: position Lucas as THE thought leader in construction/industrial technology
and leadership WITHOUT revealing the exit plan.

**Safe content angles:**
- "Here is what I built this weekend to solve a problem at work" (frames side projects as job-enhancing)
- "The construction industry is 10 years behind. Here is what I am doing about it."
- "You do not need permission to learn new skills. Here is my process."
- "Leadership is not about being the smartest person. It is about building the smartest team."
- CLARITY book content -- all of it positions him as a leader, not a leaver
- Technical tutorials that showcase expertise without revealing proprietary work
- "I still have time for family, fitness, and learning. You have the same 24 hours."

**Content to NEVER post:**
- Revenue numbers from side ventures
- Exit timeline or "leaving corporate" messaging
- Ridgeline details or client names
- Anything negative about Alimak or the corporate environment
- Screenshots of proprietary work systems

## The Core Message

Lucas does not think he is special. That is the point. He believes everyone has the
choice to learn, grow, and build -- most people just choose not to. He is not looking
down on people. He is frustrated that people sell themselves short when the tools are
right there.

The tone is not arrogant. It is: "I did this. You can too. Here is how. Stop making excuses."

## Work Ethic Reality

Lucas works at a pace that most people cannot comprehend:
- Full-time corporate VP role
- Building Ridgeline platform
- Writing and launching CLARITY
- Building Forge AI infrastructure
- Managing IAC Solutions consulting
- Learning new technology constantly
- Family time with his son Egan
- All of this simultaneously

He does not brag about this. But the content should subtly demonstrate it through
what he ships and shares, not through "I work so hard" posts. Show, do not tell.
